Atlas fail score codes (negative fail code means check not performed):

  • 1 - data files are missing
  • 2 - array design is not in Atlas
  • 3 - experiment has a type not eligible for Atlas
  • 4 - two-channel experiment
  • 5 - does not have factor values
  • 6 - experiment doesn't have replicates for at least 1 factor type
  • 7 - factor type or characteristics types are not from controlled vocabulary
  • 8 - factor type or characteristics types are repeated
  • 9 - experiment is in a GEO superseries
  • 10 - retired experiment has too many (>4) factor types
  • 11 - experiment has no factors at all
  • 12 - retired experiment has more than one source organism/species
  • 13 - units used are not in EFO
  • 14 - sequencing checks (presence of run accession, fastq URI and submitted file names (for non-GEO, non-ERAD submissions)) on files with an AE accession
  • 15 - retired experiment may have technical replicates, so not continuing to count replicates of factors
  • 16 - no label information for a microarray labeled extract
  • 17 - species mismatch between source material and ADF for at least one sample
  • 18 - Species in SDRF does not match NCBI taxonomy "scientific name".
  • 19 - Experiment has more than one organism in total.
  • 20 - Assay names or run accessions are not R-safe.
  • 21 - Technical replicate IDs do not conform to agreed convention (e.g. "group 1").
  • 22 - Experiment is present in failedCuration, failedQC, or minPvalGt0.5 directory.
  • 23 - Human disease experiment must have organism part and/or cell type and/or cell line.
  • 24 - Found technical replicate group(s) with only one assay/run.
  • 999 - Checking didn't happen at all (e.g. due to invalid MAGE-TAB).
